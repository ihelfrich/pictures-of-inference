# Pictures of Inference

*A Visual Course in Statistics, Calculus, and Econometrics*

**Working title.** The final title is open. *Reading the World in Numbers* and *The Quantitative Eye* are alternatives we will revisit when the manuscript is mature enough to know its own name.

## What this book is

A single integrated text that takes a reader from "what is a correlation" to the working knowledge needed to read, evaluate, and contribute to research published in journals like the *American Economic Review*, *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, *Journal of Econometric Theory*, and *Econometrica*.

It is a four-part book, structured as a journey:

- **Part One. How to See.** Visual literacy. Probability, distributions, descriptive statistics, the grammar of graphics, reading and critiquing quantitative communication. The reader leaves Part One able to read empirical research with comprehension.

- **Part Two. How to Speak.** Production. Estimation, inference, regression, basic causal reasoning, communication of results. The reader leaves Part Two as a competent applied analyst.

- **Part Three. How to Think.** Theoretical foundations. Calculus and linear algebra in service of statistical understanding, asymptotic theory, the geometry and philosophy of inference. The reader leaves Part Three with the apparatus that underlies the techniques.

- **Part Four. How to Discover.** The research frontier. Modern econometrics, modern causal inference, computational methods, original research methodology. The reader leaves Part Four prepared to engage with current literature.

## What makes it different

**Math as a language, taught as one.** Most quantitative books treat notation as an obstacle the reader must clear. This book treats notation as a language the reader is learning to speak. Equations are parsed before they are computed. Translation between English and mathematical expression is an explicit, drilled skill. By the end, the reader does not just calculate — they read, write, and argue in the language of mathematics.

**Visualization as a primary mode of reasoning.** Most books treat figures as decoration on top of prose. In this book, figures lead. Each concept is visualized first; prose explains the figure. The book teaches a consistent visual vocabulary — color conventions, geometric encodings, uncertainty representations — that the reader internalizes by chapter five and benefits from for the rest of the journey. Underused visualization techniques (slope graphs, hex bins, ridge plots, alluvial diagrams, hypothetical outcome plots) are given equal weight with the standard repertoire.

**Tool fluency, not tool tribalism.** The book uses R, Stata, Python, and where appropriate other tools — choosing the right one for each task and explaining why. The reader develops a portfolio of fluencies rather than commitment to a single ecosystem. This is how working analysts actually live; the book reflects it.

**Living datasets that recur.** Four real datasets thread through the book. The reader meets them in early chapters as scatter plots and frequency tables; encounters them again in later chapters as the substrate for regression, panel methods, causal estimation, and structural modeling. The datasets become familiar and the reader's skills accumulate against a stable background.

**A genuine through-line from beginner to researcher.** Most books in this space serve one audience. Wickham's *R for Data Science* is brilliant and stops well before the research frontier. Greene's *Econometric Analysis* is brilliant and assumes the reader has already arrived. This book builds the bridge.

## Audience

The primary reader is a smart, motivated person who wants to understand quantitative methods deeply but does not currently have that understanding. They might be:

- An undergraduate considering graduate study in economics, public policy, or a quantitative social science.
- A working professional in policy, journalism, or applied research who wants to move beyond consuming statistical results to producing and critiquing them.
- A self-directed learner who has been frustrated by the gap between accessible-but-shallow popular books and rigorous-but-impenetrable graduate texts.
- A graduate student who took the standard sequence, can run a regression, but does not yet feel they understand why anything works.

The book serves all of these readers because of its layered structure. A reader can engage with any chapter at the depth that suits them and return for greater depth later.

## Floor and ceiling

**Floor.** One semester of college algebra. The reader is comfortable with functions, graphs, exponents, and basic algebraic manipulation. Calculus is not assumed and is developed where needed. Linear algebra is not assumed and is developed where needed. No prior statistics is assumed.

**Ceiling.** The advanced sections of Part Four reach material currently active in the research literature. A reader who completes the book is prepared to read primary sources in current econometrics and contribute to applied or theoretical work.

## Format

The book is built in Quarto. The primary reading experience is a website — searchable, linkable, with interactive figures where they teach what static figures cannot. A complete PDF is generated for offline reading and printing. Source files live in a public GitHub repository under an open license; corrections and contributions are invited.

## Length

A first edition of approximately 50 chapters totaling roughly 800-1200 pages in print. The Quarto format makes this length less daunting than it sounds: the reader navigates by chapter, not by page count, and most chapters can be read in a single sitting.

## Comparable works

- *Statistical Rethinking* (McElreath) — voice, integration of code, depth-with-accessibility ambition. The closest spiritual cousin.
- *Mostly Harmless Econometrics* and *Mastering 'Metrics* (Angrist & Pischke) — modern empirical sensibility, applied focus.
- *R for Data Science* (Wickham & Grolemund) — production quality, format, reader experience.
- *Econometric Analysis* (Greene) — comprehensive coverage, scholarly tone, research-level treatment.
- *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information* and *Envisioning Information* (Tufte) — visual design philosophy.
- *Causal Inference: The Mixtape* (Cunningham) — accessible voice for advanced material.

This book occupies a position none of these does. It begins lower than any of them and reaches higher than most, with a sustained commitment to visualization and language as organizing principles.
